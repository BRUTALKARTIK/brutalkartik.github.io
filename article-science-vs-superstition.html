Science vs Superstition

Superstition arises when people accept claims without evidence. Science, on the other hand, demands testing, observation, and verification.

Throughout history, many superstitious beliefs—about disease, nature, and the universe—collapsed when scientific explanations emerged.

Science does not claim absolute certainty. Instead, it constantly questions itself and improves through evidence.

A rational society encourages questioning, not blind belief.
